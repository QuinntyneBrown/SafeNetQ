# L2 Requirements — SafeNetQ Community Emergency Mutual Aid Platform

---

## L1-1: User Authentication & KYC Onboarding

### L2-1.1: User Registration
Members shall register with email, phone number, and password. The system shall validate input and send a verification email/SMS.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Registration form collects name, email, phone, password
- Email verification link is sent and must be confirmed before account is active
- Phone number receives SMS verification code
- Password meets minimum complexity (8+ chars, upper, lower, digit, special)
- Duplicate email/phone is rejected with clear error message

### L2-1.2: Authentication & Session Management
The platform shall support secure login with email/password and optional MFA (TOTP or SMS). Admin/committee accounts require MFA.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Login with email and password returns JWT tokens (access + refresh)
- MFA enrollment flow for TOTP (authenticator app) and SMS
- Admin and committee roles cannot access dashboard without MFA enabled
- Sessions expire after 15 minutes of inactivity for admin accounts
- Failed login attempts trigger lockout after 5 attempts (30-minute cooldown)

### L2-1.3: KYC Identity Verification
Members shall complete identity verification by uploading government-issued ID and a selfie for facial match, with address verification.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Upload flow accepts passport, driver's license, or provincial ID card
- Selfie capture with liveness detection
- Integration with identity verification provider returns match confidence score
- Members with verified KYC have a "Verified" badge on their profile
- KYC status is stored and auditable
- Members cannot contribute or request assistance until KYC is complete

### L2-1.4: Password Reset & Account Recovery
Members shall be able to reset their password via email link and recover accounts via verified phone number.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- "Forgot Password" sends a time-limited (1 hour) reset link to registered email
- Password reset requires entering new password meeting complexity requirements
- Account recovery via SMS sends a one-time code to verified phone
- All password changes are logged in audit trail

## L1-2: Member Contribution Management

### L2-2.1: Contribution Tier Selection
Members shall choose a contribution tier ($25, $50, or $100/month) during onboarding or from their dashboard. The fee breakdown (85% trust, 10% reserve, 5% platform fee) shall be clearly displayed.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Three tier options displayed with monthly amount and breakdown
- Member can change tier from dashboard (effective next billing cycle)
- Fee breakdown shown before confirmation: trust portion, reserve portion, platform fee
- Tier change history is logged

### L2-2.2: Payment Method Setup
Members shall add and manage payment methods (PAD bank account, credit card, or Interac).
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Add bank account for PAD with institution number, transit number, account number
- Add credit card with Stripe Elements (PCI-compliant)
- Set a default payment method
- Remove or update payment methods
- Payment method details are tokenized (never stored raw)

### L2-2.3: Automated Monthly Contributions
The system shall process monthly contributions automatically on the member's billing date.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Contributions are charged on the same day each month (signup anniversary)
- Failed payments are retried up to 3 times over 7 days
- Member is notified of successful and failed payments via email/push
- After 3 consecutive failures, membership is suspended with notification
- Contribution receipt is generated for each successful payment

### L2-2.4: Contribution History
Members shall view their full contribution history with dates, amounts, status, and receipt downloads.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Paginated list of all contributions with date, amount, tier, status (success/failed/pending)
- Filter by date range and status
- Download individual receipts as PDF
- Export full history as CSV

## L1-3: Assistance Request & Review Workflow

### L2-3.1: Request Submission
Eligible members shall submit an assistance request by selecting an emergency category, providing a description, and uploading supporting documentation.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Form shows eligible categories: Medical, Housing, Income Loss, Death in Family, Domestic Crisis
- Description field (max 2000 characters)
- Document upload supporting multiple files (PDF, JPG, PNG, max 10MB each, max 5 files)
- System validates eligibility: KYC complete, waiting period met, contributions current, not exceeding payout frequency limit
- Ineligible members see a clear explanation of why they cannot submit
- Submission generates a unique request ID and confirmation notification

### L2-3.2: Committee Review Dashboard
Committee members shall view pending requests, review documentation, and cast approval/denial votes.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- List of pending requests with submission date, category, and member ID (anonymized)
- Detail view shows description, uploaded documents (viewed in-browser), and member standing
- Committee member can vote Approve or Deny with required justification text
- Conflict of interest: committee member is auto-recused if request is from their community group
- Majority vote (3 of 5) determines outcome
- Voting deadline: 48 hours from assignment

### L2-3.3: Request Status Tracking
Members shall see real-time status of their request: Submitted, Under Review, Approved, Denied, Paid.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Status timeline visualization showing each stage with timestamps
- Push/email notification at each status change
- If denied, member sees the reason (category-level, not detailed committee notes)
- Estimated payout date shown when approved

### L2-3.4: Appeals Process
Members shall be able to appeal a denied request within 14 days, reviewed by a separate panel.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- "Appeal" button visible on denied requests within 14-day window
- Appeal form allows additional documentation and explanation
- Appeal is assigned to a different panel than original reviewers
- Appeal decision is final and binding
- Member is notified of appeal outcome via email and push notification

## L1-4: Payout Processing

### L2-4.1: Payout Execution
Approved requests shall trigger a payout via direct deposit or Interac e-Transfer within 48-72 hours.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Payout is initiated automatically upon committee approval
- Member selects payout method: direct deposit (to verified bank account) or Interac e-Transfer
- Payout amount respects per-event maximum ($2,000-$5,000 based on tier)
- Payout is drawn from Member Trust Account only
- Payout confirmation sent to member with amount and reference number
- Transaction is logged in audit trail with timestamp, amount, method, and approving committee members

### L2-4.2: Payout Limits Enforcement
The system shall enforce per-event maximums, annual maximums per member, and payout frequency limits.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Per-event cap enforced based on member tier
- Annual maximum per member tracked and enforced (rolling 12-month window)
- Maximum 2 payouts per member per 12-month period
- Remaining annual limit displayed on member dashboard
- System prevents submission if member has exhausted annual limit

## L1-5: Member Dashboard

### L2-5.1: Dashboard Overview
Members shall see a summary dashboard with key metrics: membership standing, total contributed, next payment date, payout eligibility status, and fund health indicator.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Card-based layout showing: membership status (Active/Suspended/Pending KYC), total contributed to date, next payment date and amount, payout eligibility (eligible/waiting period/ineligible with reason)
- Fund health indicator showing fund reserve level (healthy/caution/low) without revealing exact balance
- Quick action buttons: Make Contribution, Request Assistance, View History

### L2-5.2: Contribution History View
Members shall access detailed contribution history with filtering, sorting, and export capabilities.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Same as L2-2.4

### L2-5.3: Payout History View
Members shall view their payout history with dates, amounts, categories, and statuses.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- List of all past payouts with date, amount, emergency category, and status
- Detail view shows request summary and timeline
- No payouts shows a "No payouts yet" empty state

### L2-5.4: Profile Management
Members shall view and edit their profile information, notification preferences, and language settings.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Edit name, phone number, address (triggers re-verification if address changes)
- Update notification preferences (email, push, SMS toggles per notification type)
- Select preferred language
- View and download personal data (PIPEDA right of access)
- Request account deletion (with contribution/payout implications explained)

## L1-6: Community Feed & Notifications

### L2-6.1: Anonymized Community Feed
The platform shall display a feed showing anonymized assistance events to build community trust.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Feed entries show: "A member was helped with a [category] emergency" with date and amount range (e.g., "$1,000-$2,000")
- No personally identifiable information is displayed
- Feed is paginated with infinite scroll
- Optional: member who received help can choose to share a thank-you message (opt-in, reviewed for PII)

### L2-6.2: Notification System
Members shall receive notifications via email, push, and SMS for key events.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Notification events: contribution receipt, payment failure, request status change, community announcement, payout processed
- Each notification type can be toggled per channel (email/push/SMS) in preferences
- Push notifications work on web and mobile
- Email notifications use branded templates
- SMS is reserved for critical alerts (payment failure, payout processed)

## L1-7: Document Vault

### L2-7.1: Secure Document Upload & Storage
Members shall upload documents encrypted at rest with AES-256, stored in Canadian data centers, with time-limited access URLs.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Upload supports PDF, JPG, PNG up to 10MB per file
- Files are encrypted at rest with AES-256 in Canadian-region cloud storage
- Access URLs expire after 1 hour
- Only the member, assigned committee reviewers, and admins with audit justification can access documents
- Documents are retained per FINTRAC requirements (5 years) then auto-deleted
- Upload progress indicator shown during upload

## L1-8: Admin & Governance Dashboard

### L2-8.1: Fund Health Dashboard
Admins and treasurers shall view real-time fund metrics: trust account balance, reserve ratio, contribution inflow, payout outflow, and runway projections.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Dashboard cards: Trust Account balance, Reserve Account balance, Operating Account balance
- Charts: Monthly contribution inflow (bar), Monthly payout outflow (bar), Reserve ratio trend (line)
- Runway projection: months of payouts remaining at current rate
- Alerts when reserve ratio drops below 3 months
- Data refreshes in real-time or near-real-time (within 5 minutes)

### L2-8.2: Member Management
Admins shall view, search, and manage member profiles including contribution status, payout history, and suspension/removal.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Searchable, paginated member list with filters (status, tier, KYC status, join date)
- Member detail view: profile info, contribution history, payout history, KYC status
- Actions: suspend member (with reason), reactivate member, remove member (with reason, triggers notification)
- All admin actions are logged in audit trail

### L2-8.3: AML/KYC Management
Compliance officers shall manage KYC verification statuses, flag suspicious transactions, and generate FINTRAC reports.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Queue of pending KYC verifications with auto-verification results
- Manual override for KYC approval/rejection
- Suspicious transaction flagging with reason codes
- Generate STR (Suspicious Transaction Report) for FINTRAC submission
- Generate Large Cash Transaction Reports for transactions >= $10,000
- Dashboard showing compliance metrics: pending KYC count, flagged transactions, report filing status

### L2-8.4: Audit Trail
The platform shall maintain an immutable log of all fund movements, committee decisions, admin actions, and data access events.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Every event logged with: timestamp, user ID, action type, target entity, details, IP address
- Audit log is append-only (no deletions or modifications)
- Searchable and filterable by date range, user, action type
- Exportable for external audit purposes (CSV, JSON)
- Retained for minimum 7 years

### L2-8.5: Role-Based Access Control
The platform shall enforce RBAC with roles: Admin, Committee Member, Treasurer, Auditor, Compliance Officer, Member.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Each role has defined permissions (view, create, edit, delete, approve) per resource
- Users can have multiple roles
- Role assignment requires admin privileges
- Permission changes take effect immediately
- All role changes are logged in audit trail

## L1-9: Fund Financial Governance

### L2-9.1: Account Separation
The system shall enforce separation of Member Trust Account, Operating Account, and Reserve Account in all financial operations.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Contribution splits are automatically calculated and routed (85% trust, 10% reserve, 5% operating)
- Payouts can only be drawn from the Member Trust Account
- Operating expenses can only be drawn from the Operating Account
- Reserve Account has separate balance tracking
- Monthly reconciliation report comparing expected vs actual balances

### L2-9.2: Waiting Period Enforcement
New members shall complete a configurable waiting period (default 3 months) before becoming eligible for payouts.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Waiting period countdown visible on member dashboard
- System blocks assistance request submission during waiting period
- Waiting period is configurable by admin (default: 3 months)
- Waiting period resets if membership lapses (2+ consecutive missed payments)

### L2-9.3: Payout Cap Management
Admins shall configure per-event and annual payout caps per contribution tier.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Admin UI to set per-event max and annual max per tier
- Changes apply to future requests only (not retroactive)
- Current caps displayed to members on assistance request form
- System enforces caps at submission and approval time

## L1-10: Compliance & Reporting

### L2-10.1: FINTRAC Reporting
The platform shall generate and manage FINTRAC-required reports: STRs, Large Cash Transaction Reports, and Terrorist Property Reports.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Automated flagging of transactions >= $10,000 for Large Cash Transaction Report
- Suspicious activity detection rules (e.g., structuring, rapid in/out)
- STR creation workflow with required fields per FINTRAC specifications
- Report export in FINTRAC-accepted format
- Filing status tracking (draft, filed, acknowledged)

### L2-10.2: Financial Reports
The platform shall generate monthly and annual financial reports with fund utilization, payout statistics, and demographic breakdowns.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Monthly report: contributions collected, payouts disbursed, fund balances, reserve ratio
- Annual report: all monthly data aggregated, year-over-year comparison, demographic breakdown (anonymized)
- Reports downloadable as PDF
- Auto-generated on schedule with email delivery to authorized roles

### L2-10.3: Privacy & Data Management
The platform shall comply with PIPEDA requirements including data access requests, deletion requests, breach notification, and consent management.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Members can download all their personal data (JSON/CSV export)
- Members can request account deletion (with contribution implications displayed)
- Deletion requests processed within 30 days
- Data breach notification workflow: detect, assess, notify Commissioner and affected members
- Consent records maintained for all data collection purposes
- Privacy policy accessible from all screens

## L1-11: Accessibility & Multi-Language Support

### L2-11.1: WCAG 2.0 AA Compliance
All UI components shall meet WCAG 2.0 Level AA accessibility standards.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- All interactive elements are keyboard navigable
- Screen reader compatible (proper ARIA labels, roles, and landmarks)
- Color contrast ratio meets 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text
- Form fields have associated labels and error messages are announced
- Focus indicators are visible on all interactive elements
- No content relies solely on color to convey information

### L2-11.2: Multi-Language Support
The platform shall support English, French, and configurable additional community languages.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Language selector in header/settings
- All static UI text available in English and French at launch
- Framework supports adding new languages via translation files (i18n)
- Date, currency, and number formatting adapts to locale
- RTL layout support for future Arabic/Hebrew/Urdu languages

## L1-12: Responsive UI Design

### L2-12.1: Responsive Layout System
All screens shall render correctly across breakpoints: x-small (<576px), small (576-767px), medium (768-991px), large (992-1199px), x-large (>=1200px).
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Navigation collapses to hamburger menu on x-small and small screens
- Dashboard cards reflow from multi-column (large) to single-column (x-small)
- Tables switch to card-based layout on small screens
- Forms maintain usability and readability at all breakpoints
- No horizontal scroll at any breakpoint
- Touch targets are minimum 44x44px on mobile breakpoints

### L2-12.2: UI Component Library
The platform shall have a consistent component library including buttons, inputs, dialogs, toasts, cards, tables, navigation, and status indicators.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Primary, secondary, and destructive button variants with hover/focus/disabled states
- Input fields with labels, placeholders, validation states (error, success, warning)
- Modal dialogs with overlay, title, content, and action buttons
- Toast notifications for success, error, warning, and info with auto-dismiss
- Card components with header, body, and footer sections
- Data tables with sorting, pagination, and responsive card fallback
- Status badges (Active, Pending, Suspended, Verified, etc.)
- Consistent spacing, typography, and color system

### L2-12.3: Screen Designs — Onboarding Flow
Design all onboarding screens: Welcome/Landing, Registration, Email Verification, KYC Upload, KYC Pending, Tier Selection, Payment Setup, Onboarding Complete.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Each screen designed for all 5 breakpoints
- Progress indicator showing current step
- Clear CTAs and form validation feedback
- Accessible and visually consistent with design system

### L2-12.4: Screen Designs — Member Screens
Design all member-facing screens: Dashboard, Contribution History, Request Assistance, Request Status, Payout History, Community Feed, Document Vault, Profile/Settings.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Each screen designed for all 5 breakpoints
- Consistent navigation and layout structure
- Empty states for lists with no data
- Loading states for async data

### L2-12.5: Screen Designs — Admin Screens
Design all admin screens: Committee Review, Fund Health Dashboard, Member Management, AML/KYC Management, Audit Trail, Reports, Settings.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Each screen designed for all 5 breakpoints
- Data-dense layouts optimized for desktop but usable on tablet
- Filter and search patterns consistent across admin views
- Action confirmations for destructive operations

## L1-14: Backend API & Infrastructure

### L2-14.1: API Architecture
The backend shall follow Clean Architecture with .NET 8+, providing RESTful APIs with OpenAPI documentation.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Clean Architecture layers: Domain, Application, Infrastructure, API
- RESTful endpoints with consistent naming and HTTP status codes
- OpenAPI/Swagger documentation auto-generated
- API versioning (URL prefix: /api/v1/)
- Request/response validation with proper error responses
- Rate limiting per endpoint and per user

### L2-14.2: Database Design
PostgreSQL database with proper schema design, migrations, and data encryption.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Entity Framework Core with code-first migrations
- PII fields encrypted at rest (AES-256)
- Proper indexing for common query patterns
- Soft-delete pattern for regulatory retention requirements
- Database connection pooling configured
- Automated daily backups with 30-day retention

### L2-14.3: Authentication & Authorization Service
Backend shall implement JWT-based authentication with refresh tokens and RBAC authorization.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- JWT access tokens (15-minute expiry) and refresh tokens (7-day expiry)
- Role-based authorization middleware
- Token revocation on password change or account suspension
- Brute force protection (rate limiting on auth endpoints)
- Audit logging of all auth events

### L2-14.4: Security & Infrastructure
The platform shall implement TLS 1.3, data residency in Canada, monitoring, and disaster recovery.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- TLS 1.3 enforced on all endpoints
- Deployed to Azure Canada Central or AWS ca-central-1
- Application monitoring and alerting (health checks, error rates, latency)
- RTO: 4 hours, RPO: 1 hour
- Automated daily encrypted backups
- CI/CD pipeline with automated tests, linting, and security scanning

## L1-15: Integration Services

### L2-15.1: Payment Processing Integration
Integrate with Stripe (or Moneris) for PAD contributions, credit card payments, and Interac e-Transfer payouts.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Stripe Connect or equivalent for fund separation
- PAD setup and recurring billing
- Credit card tokenization (PCI-compliant via Stripe Elements)
- Interac e-Transfer payout initiation
- Webhook handling for payment events (success, failure, dispute)
- Retry logic for failed payments

### L2-15.2: Identity Verification Integration
Integrate with Jumio (or Onfido) for document-based identity verification and facial match.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- SDK integration for document capture and selfie
- Webhook processing for verification results
- Store verification status and confidence score
- Handle retry flow for failed verifications
- Fallback to manual review for edge cases

### L2-15.3: Notification Service Integration
Integrate with SendGrid (email) and Twilio (SMS) for transactional notifications.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Email templates for all notification types (contribution receipt, status updates, etc.)
- SMS delivery for critical alerts
- Delivery status tracking
- Unsubscribe handling per CAN-SPAM/CASL compliance
- Rate limiting to prevent notification spam

### L2-15.4: Document Storage Integration
Integrate with AWS S3 or Azure Blob Storage (Canadian region) for encrypted document storage.
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Server-side encryption with AES-256
- Pre-signed URLs with 1-hour expiry for document access
- Lifecycle policies for auto-deletion after retention period (5 years)
- Virus/malware scanning on upload
- Storage in ca-central-1 or Canada Central region only
